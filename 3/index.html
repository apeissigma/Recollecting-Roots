How does one heal from something so deeply ingrained in their own body? For my family, returning to Cambodia was a step in the right direction. While my cousins and I carry the weight of this trauma in different ways, seeing our mothers reconnect together in the land they were born in was the clarity we needed. Healing is still very much about listening and learning, both inward and outward. I'm grateful for the other opportunities I've had to learn from others as well. During a memorial held for the 50th anniversary of the genocide at the National Cambodian Heritage Museum & Killing Fields Memorial, I was able to connect with many second-generation immigrants with similar stories, some of whom are practicing trauma therapists and educators. Strong communities like the Cambodian Association of Illinois are built to lift each other, elevating the voices of survivors and honoring the silenced. In this sense, healing is a collective effort. While we have our ups and downs, my family's bond is a testament to resilience and strength. ■
